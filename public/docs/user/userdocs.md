# Wiki

The Wiki is your personal and shared knowledge space inside Rimori. Create pages to save notes, grammar rules, study summaries, or anything else useful — and publish them to share with other learners in your community.

---

## Getting Started

Open the Wiki plugin from your Rimori dashboard. The left sidebar shows a page tree. If you are new, the tree is empty — click **New Page** to create your first page.

---

## Creating Pages

Click **New Page** at the top of the page tree to create a root-level page.

To create a **subpage** (nested under an existing page), right-click any page in the tree and select **Add subpage**.

Each page has:

- **Icon** — An emoji or symbol shown in the tree (e.g. 📖, ⚙, ★) — makes pages easier to identify at a glance
- **Title** — The page name, shown in the tree and as a heading
- **Description** — An optional subtitle displayed below the title
- **Content** — The main body, written in markdown

---

## Editing Pages

Click the **edit button** (pencil icon) on any page to open the editor. The toolbar provides formatting options:

- **Text:** Bold, italic, strikethrough, inline code
- **Structure:** Paragraphs, headings (H1–H3)
- **Lists:** Bullet lists, numbered lists
- **Blocks:** Code blocks, blockquotes
- **Media:** Insert images
- **Transform:** Select text and click the sparkle icon to transform it (rephrase, simplify, expand, etc.)
- **Utilities:** Copy content as markdown, toggle fullscreen editor

Click **Save** when done, or **Cancel** to discard changes.

---

## Organizing Pages

Pages are arranged in a tree structure in the left sidebar. You can:

- **Create subpages** — right-click a page → "Add subpage"
- **Move a page** — right-click → "Move", then choose a new parent
- **Reorder pages** — pages are ordered by their sort order, which is set when they are created or moved
- **Delete a page** — right-click → "Delete"

The **breadcrumb trail** at the top of each page shows the full path from the root. Click any breadcrumb to navigate up.

---

## Publishing Pages

All pages start as **private** — only you can see them. There are three visibility levels:

- **Private** — only you can see the page
- **Guild** — shared with members of your guild/group
- **Public** — visible to all Rimori learners

To publish, right-click the page in the tree and choose **Publish in Guild** or **Publish for All**, or use the globe icon on the page viewer. To make a published page private again, right-click it → **Unpublish**.

Use the **My Pages** and **Public** tabs at the top of the sidebar to switch between your private pages and all published pages.

---

## Comments and Reactions

Below each page there is a comments section:

- **Write a comment** — type in the text area and click "Comment"
- **React with an emoji** — click an existing reaction to add yours, or click "+" to choose from: 👍 ❤️ 😄 🎉 🤔 👀
- **Delete your own comments** — use the trash icon next to your comment

Comments are a good way to ask questions on public pages or leave notes for yourself on private ones.

---

## Sidebar Access

The Wiki is also available from the **side panel**. Click the Wiki icon to browse pages without leaving the current plugin. Click any page to navigate to it in the main wiki view.

This is useful when you want to quickly reference a note while working in another plugin — for example, checking a grammar rule you saved while doing a writing exercise.

---

## How It Connects to Other Plugins

- **Grammar Assistant:** When the Grammar Assistant gives you a useful explanation, you can save it to the Wiki for future reference.
- **Study Plan:** The Study Plan can assign wiki pages as reading exercises. When assigned, you are navigated directly to the page.
- **Context menu:** The Wiki sidebar is accessible from any plugin, so you can check your notes without leaving what you are doing.

---

## Settings

The Wiki plugin uses your global skill levels from Settings → Skills for page visibility filtering. There are no plugin-specific settings.

---

## Tips & Life Hacks

- **Use the right-click menu for everything.** Add subpages, move, delete, publish, or unpublish — all from the right-click context menu on any page in the tree.
- **Give top-level pages meaningful icons.** A tree with 📖 Grammar, 🧠 Vocabulary, 📝 Notes is much faster to scan than one with no icons.
- **Use subpages to organize by topic.** For example, a "Grammar" root page with subpages for "Verb Tenses", "Articles", "Word Order" keeps related notes together.
- **Save Grammar Assistant explanations here.** When the assistant gives you a helpful breakdown of a grammar rule, copy it into a wiki page. Over time you build your own personalized grammar reference.
- **Browse public pages for inspiration.** Other learners publish notes, summaries, and explanations. Check the Public tab to see what the community has created — you might find exactly the reference you need.

---

## As You Progress

Public wiki pages can be **skill-gated** — a page author can set a minimum CEFR level requirement for a specific skill (reading, writing, grammar, speaking, listening, or understanding). If your level is below the requirement, the page is hidden from your view. This filtering only applies to public pages — your own private pages are always visible to you.

This means:

- As your skills improve, new pages become visible
- Advanced learners see a richer library of community content
- Page authors can target their content to the right audience

## FAQ

**Q: Can other people edit my pages?**
A: No — only you can edit your own pages, even if they are published. Others can view and comment on published pages.

**Q: What happens when I publish a page?**
A: It becomes visible to all Rimori users in the Public tab. You can unpublish it at any time to make it private again.

**Q: Can the Study Plan assign wiki pages as exercises?**
A: Yes — the Study Plan can assign specific wiki pages as reading exercises.

**Q: Why can I not see some public pages?**
A: Some pages have a minimum skill level requirement. As your skills improve, more pages become visible.

## Troubleshooting

**Pages not loading:**
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page

**Cannot find a page you created:**
- Check the **My Pages** tab (not Public)
- Make sure you are looking at the correct level in the tree — it might be nested as a subpage
